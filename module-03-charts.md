# Module 03 — Spotting Trends & Timing Your Entries

**Duration:** Week 5–7 | ~3 hours total  
**Goal:** Read a price chart. Identify a good entry point. Stop buying at the top.

---

## 3.1 Why Charts Matter

Price charts are not magic. They reflect human behavior — fear, greed, and momentum. Learning to read them helps you:

- Buy when others are fearful (good prices)
- Avoid chasing stocks that have already run up 30–50%
- Set logical exit points before you enter a trade

---

## 3.2 Candlestick Charts — The Basics

Every candlestick represents one time period (day, hour, week — you choose).

```
      │  ← Upper wick (high of the period)
    ┌─┴─┐
    │   │  ← Body (open to close)
    └─┬─┘
      │  ← Lower wick (low of the period)
```

- **Green candle:** Closing price was higher than opening price. Buyers won.
- **Red candle:** Closing price was lower than opening price. Sellers won.
- **Long wicks:** Show rejection — the price tried to go there but got pushed back.

---

## 3.3 Support and Resistance

**Support** is a price level where a stock tends to stop falling and bounce back. Buyers step in at support because they see value.

**Resistance** is a price level where a stock tends to stop rising and pull back. Sellers appear at resistance to take profits.

**Why these levels matter:**
- Buying near support with a tight stop loss below it = good risk/reward
- Buying near resistance = you are buying where sellers are waiting

**How to find them:** Look at a 6-month or 1-year chart. Find price levels where the stock has bounced or reversed multiple times. Those are your support and resistance zones.

---

## 3.4 Trend Direction — Trade With It, Not Against It

A trend is a series of price moves in one direction.

| Trend Type | Pattern | What It Means |
|------------|---------|---------------|
| **Uptrend** | Higher highs and higher lows | Buyers are in control. Look for buying opportunities on pullbacks. |
| **Downtrend** | Lower highs and lower lows | Sellers are in control. Avoid buying. Wait for reversal confirmation. |
| **Sideways** | Price bouncing between a range | No clear winner. Can trade the range or wait for a breakout. |

## 3.5 Three Indicators Worth Learning

### Moving Averages (MA)

A moving average smooths out price noise by averaging the closing price over N days.

- **50-day MA:** Medium-term trend. Price above = bullish.
- **200-day MA:** Long-term trend. Price above = strongly bullish. Price crossing below = major warning.

**Golden Cross:** The 50-day MA crosses above the 200-day MA. Historically a strong bullish signal.  
**Death Cross:** The 50-day MA crosses below the 200-day MA. Historically a bearish warning.

### RSI — Relative Strength Index

Measures momentum on a scale of 0–100.

| RSI Level | Interpretation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Below 30 | Oversold. Stock may be due for a bounce. Potential buy signal. |
| 30–70 | Neutral zone. No strong signal either way. |
| Above 70 | Overbought. Stock may be due for a pullback. Be cautious buying here. |

RSI alone is not enough. Use it to confirm other signals — not as a standalone buy/sell trigger.

### MACD — Moving Average Convergence Divergence

Shows when a stock is gaining or losing momentum.

- **MACD line crosses above signal line:** Bullish momentum building.
- **MACD line crosses below signal line:** Bearish momentum building.
- **Histogram growing:** Momentum is accelerating in that direction.

You do not need to understand the math. Learn to read the signal.

## 3.6 Entry and Exit Rules — Plan Before You Buy

One of the most common beginner mistakes is having no plan before entering a trade. Before every single trade, answer these four questions in writing:

1. **Why am I buying this?** (Your thesis — what has to happen for this trade to work?)
2. **At what price does my thesis break?** (Your stop loss — where you exit if wrong.)
3. **What is my target price?** (Your exit — where you take profit.)
4. **How much of my account am I risking?** (Your position size.)

Write these down in `progress-tracker.md` before placing the order. This is not optional.

---

## 3.7 The 2% Rule — Protecting Your Capital

Never risk more than 2% of your total account on a single trade.

**With $750, that is $15 of maximum loss per trade.**

**How to calculate position size:**
1. Decide your entry price. (Example: $10.00)
2. Decide your stop loss price. (Example: $9.50)
3. Risk per share = $10.00 - $9.50 = $0.50
4. Max shares = $15 ÷ $0.50 = 30 shares
5. Total position = 30 × $10.00 = $300

This keeps you in the game long enough to learn and improve.
