## 2.1 The Metrics That Actually Matter

When you pull up a stock on Yahoo Finance, Wealthsimple, or Google Finance, you will see many numbers. These are the ones that matter:

| Metric | What It Means | What to Look For |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **P/E Ratio** | Price divided by earnings per share. What you pay for $1 of profit. | Under 20 is reasonable. Over 40 means the market expects high future growth. Compare to industry peers. |
| **Revenue Growth** | Is the company making more money each year? | Look for 10%+ year-over-year growth for 2+ consecutive years. |
| **EPS (Earnings Per Share)** | Company profit divided by shares outstanding. | Rising EPS over time = healthy company. Negative EPS means the company is not yet profitable. |
| **Debt-to-Equity** | How much debt vs. shareholder equity. | Below 1.0 is generally healthy. High debt is dangerous when interest rates are high. |
| **Free Cash Flow (FCF)** | Cash left over after running the business. | Positive FCF means the company can survive downturns and fund growth without borrowing. |

## 2.2 Two Schools of Analysis

### Fundamental Analysis
- Asks: Is this a good **company**?
- Looks at financials, business model, competitive advantages, industry trends.
- Best for holds of 3–12+ months.
- Think: Warren Buffett, Peter Lynch.

### Technical Analysis
- Asks: Is this a good **time** to buy?
- Looks at price charts, patterns, momentum indicators.
- Best for timing entries and exits.
- Covered in depth in Module 03.

**To grow your portfolio actively, you need both.** Fundamentals tell you what to buy. Technicals tell you when.

## 2.3 The 5-Point Stock Screen

Use this checklist before buying any individual stock. Run it in order.

1. Is revenue growing year-over-year for the last 2+ years?
2. Is the P/E ratio reasonable for the industry? (Compare the stock to 2–3 competitors.)
3. Does the company have a clear competitive advantage? (Brand, patents, network effects, switching costs.)
4. Is the stock down 20–40% from its 52-week high with no fundamental reason for the decline?
5. What is the analyst consensus? (Yahoo Finance → Analysis tab → Recommendations.)

**Scoring:** If you answer YES to 4 out of 5, the stock is worth deeper research. If 2 or fewer, move on.

## 2.4 Evaluating Your Current Holdings

### XEQT — iShares All-Equity ETF Portfolio

**What you own:** A single ETF that holds thousands of stocks across Canada (~25%), US (~45%), international developed (~25%), and emerging markets (~5%).

**Verdict:** This is one of the best "set and forget" ETFs available in Canada. It is broadly diversified, low cost (MER 0.20%), and rebalances automatically. There is no reason to sell it. Consider it the stable core of your portfolio.

**One thing to know:** Because XEQT already holds US large-cap stocks, your VFV position is adding extra US exposure on top. Decide if that is intentional.

### VFV — Vanguard S&P 500 Index ETF

**What you own:** Pure exposure to the S&P 500 — the 500 largest US companies (Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Amazon, etc.).

**Verdict:** Strong long-term performer. The S&P 500 has returned ~10% annually over the long run, with periods of much higher returns. Keeping VFV alongside XEQT means you believe US large-cap will continue to outperform the rest of the world. That is a reasonable bet.

**Consider:** If you want to reduce overlap, you could consolidate into one or the other. Or keep both if you want to intentionally overweight US equities.

### LSPD — Lightspeed Commerce

**What you own:** A Canadian tech company providing cloud-based point-of-sale, e-commerce, and payment solutions to retailers and restaurants globally.

**Run the 5-Point Screen on LSPD:**
1. Revenue growing YoY? — Check recent quarterly earnings.
2. P/E reasonable? — LSPD has historically been unprofitable or marginally profitable. Look at Price-to-Sales instead.
3. Competitive advantage? — Cloud POS is competitive. Lightspeed competes with Shopify (POS), Square/Block, and Toast. Evaluate if they have a moat.
4. Down from 52-week high? — Check the chart.
5. Analyst consensus? — Check Yahoo Finance.

**Key risk:** LSPD has been through significant volatility and a short-seller report in 2021. It has pivoted toward profitability but remains a higher-risk, higher-conviction pick. You should have a clear thesis for why you are holding it.

## 2.7 Sectors Worth Watching for Growth

Not all sectors grow at the same rate. For active portfolio growth, these sectors have historically outperformed:

- **Technology** (AI, cloud, semiconductors) — you already have exposure via XEQT, VFV, and LSPD
- **Energy** (particularly Canadian oil and gas if commodity prices are rising)
- **Financials** (Canadian banks are reliable dividend payers and trend with interest rates)
- **Healthcare/Biotech** (high risk, high reward — smaller allocation)
