## 5.2 What Actually Drives Crypto Prices

Understanding why crypto moves is more important than understanding how to buy it.

| Driver | What to Know |
|--------|-------------|
| **Bitcoin dominance** | BTC price sets the mood for all crypto. When BTC rises strongly, altcoins tend to follow. When BTC drops hard, altcoins drop harder. Always watch BTC first. |
| **Macro conditions** | Crypto is a risk-on asset. It rises when investors feel optimistic and rates are falling. It falls when rates rise or risk appetite drops. |
| **Bitcoin halving cycles** | Bitcoin's supply issuance cuts in half roughly every 4 years. Historically the 12–18 months after a halving have produced strong bull markets. The most recent halving was in April 2024. Know where you are in the cycle. |
| **Narrative cycles** | Crypto runs on narratives: AI tokens, DeFi, NFTs, layer-2 solutions. Understanding what narrative is currently dominant helps time entries into relevant assets. |
| **Institutional adoption** | Bitcoin ETFs (like IBIT, FBTC) launched in the US in early 2024, bringing institutional money into the market. This is a structural change from previous cycles. |
| **Liquidity** | Low-cap coins can move 50% in days. That is opportunity and danger in equal measure. Stick to large caps until you have experience. |

## 5.3 Evaluating Your Bitcoin Position

Ask yourself these questions about your current BTC holding:

1. **When did you buy?** What was your entry price? Are you up or down?
2. **Why did you buy?** Was it a conviction play or curiosity? Your thesis matters for deciding what to do next.
3. **What is your time horizon?** Are you holding for 1 year? 5 years? This changes your strategy entirely.
4. **What percentage of your total portfolio is crypto?** If your total portfolio is ~$2,000 and you have $200 in BTC, that is 10% — a reasonable crypto allocation.

Record your answers in `progress-tracker.md`.

## 5.4 Should You Expand Beyond Bitcoin?

### Ethereum (ETH)

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **What it is** | The second-largest cryptocurrency. Powers smart contracts, DeFi, and most of the crypto application layer. |
| **Why consider it** | Real utility beyond "store of value." More volatile than BTC = more upside potential (and more risk). |
| **Risk** | Competitive pressure from Solana and other chains. Complex upcoming upgrades. |

### A Simple Crypto Allocation (if you choose to expand)

| Asset | Allocation | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| **Bitcoin (BTC)** | 60–70% | The safest crypto. Largest by market cap. Your anchor. |
| **Ethereum (ETH)** | 20–30% | Real utility, strong ecosystem. Proven track record. |
| **1 altcoin (optional)** | 10% max | Only if you can explain what it does in one sentence. Higher risk. |

Do not buy a crypto because someone on Reddit, TikTok, or Discord told you to. That is how people lose money.

## 5.5 Where to Buy and Hold Crypto — Your Options

### Option A: Wealthsimple Crypto (Simplest)
- Buy and sell crypto directly in your Wealthsimple account.
- No need for a separate exchange or wallet.
- 1.5–2% spread on trades (higher than dedicated exchanges).
- 50+ coins available including BTC, ETH, SOL.
- **Best for:** Keeping everything in one place. Simplicity over cost optimization.

### Option B: Dedicated Canadian Exchange

| Platform | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| **Newton (newton.co)** | Canadian, no trading fees (they make money on the spread). E-transfer deposits. Clean interface. |
| **Coinbase** | US-based, widely trusted, clean interface. Slightly higher fees. |
| **Kraken** | Lower fees, more coins, better for intermediate traders. |

### Option C: Bitcoin ETF in Your TFSA or RRSP
- You can buy Bitcoin exposure through an ETF without holding actual crypto.
- **BTCX** (CI Galaxy Bitcoin ETF) or **EBIT** (Evolve Bitcoin ETF) trade on the TSX.
- Advantage: Holds in your TFSA = tax-free Bitcoin gains. No crypto exchange needed.
- Disadvantage: MER fees (0.40–0.75%), cannot transfer to a wallet, and only available during market hours.

**If your BTC is already on a crypto exchange:** That is fine. You do not need to move it. Just be aware of the alternatives.

## 5.6 Risk Management Rules for Crypto

These are not suggestions. Apply all of them.

1. **Keep crypto at 10–20% of your total portfolio maximum.** With a ~$2,000 portfolio, that is $200–400 in crypto.

2. **Dollar-cost average (DCA) in.** If adding to your position, do not put your entire allocation in on one day. Buy in 3–4 equal purchases spread over 2–4 weeks.

3. **Set a hard mental stop.** If your crypto position drops 30–40% from your entry and the fundamental reason you bought it has changed, reassess and consider exiting.

4. **Crypto gains are taxable in Canada (outside registered accounts).** The CRA treats crypto as a commodity. Gains are capital gains (50% inclusion rate). Every time you sell crypto for a profit, you have a taxable event. Keep records.

5. **Use Koinly.io for tax tracking.** Connect your exchange accounts. It automatically calculates your adjusted cost base and capital gains for tax filing.

6. **Consider a Bitcoin ETF in your TFSA.** If you want additional BTC exposure, buying a Bitcoin ETF in your TFSA makes the gains completely tax-free. This is a uniquely Canadian advantage.

## 5.7 How to Research a Crypto Project

Before buying any altcoin beyond BTC and ETH, answer these questions:

1. What problem does this blockchain or token actually solve?
2. Who built it? What is the team's background?
3. What is the total supply? Is there a cap? (Unlimited supply = inflation risk.)
4. What is the market cap? (Smaller market cap = more room to grow, more risk of collapse.)
5. Is there real adoption? Are people actually using this network?

If you cannot answer all five questions confidently, do not buy it.
