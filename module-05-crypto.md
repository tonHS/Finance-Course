## 5.1 The Role of Crypto in This Strategy

Crypto is the highest-risk, highest-potential-return asset in this course. It should represent no more than **10–20% of your total investable capital** — meaning $75–$150 of your $750.

Use it for asymmetric upside. It is not your primary strategy. It is a calculated high-risk bet on one part of a diversified approach.

## 5.2 What Actually Drives Crypto Prices

Understanding why crypto moves is more important than understanding how to buy it.

| Driver | What to Know |
|--------|-------------|
| **Bitcoin dominance** | BTC price sets the mood for all crypto. When BTC rises strongly, altcoins tend to follow. When BTC drops hard, altcoins drop harder. Always watch BTC first. |
| **Macro conditions** | Crypto is a risk-on asset. It rises when investors feel optimistic and rates are falling. It falls when rates rise or risk appetite drops. |
| **Bitcoin halving cycles** | Bitcoin's supply issuance cuts in half roughly every 4 years. Historically the 12–18 months after a halving have produced strong bull markets. Know where you are in the cycle. |
| **Narrative cycles** | Crypto runs on narratives: AI tokens, DeFi, NFTs, layer-2 solutions. Understanding what narrative is currently dominant helps time entries into relevant assets. |
| **Liquidity** | Low-cap coins can move 50% in days. That is opportunity and danger in equal measure. Stick to large caps until you have experience. |

## 5.3 A Simple Beginner Crypto Allocation

For your $75–$150 crypto allocation:

| Asset | Allocation | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| **Bitcoin (BTC)** | 50% | The safest crypto. Largest by market cap. Moves less wildly than altcoins. Your anchor. |
| **Ethereum (ETH)** | 30% | The second largest. Real utility — powers smart contracts, DeFi, NFTs. Proven track record. |
| **1–2 altcoins** | 20% | Higher risk. Only buy projects you can explain in one sentence. Examples: SOL (Solana), LINK (Chainlink). |

Do not buy a crypto because someone on Reddit, TikTok, or a Discord told you to. That is how people lose money. Research the project yourself.

## 5.5 Risk Management Rules for Crypto

These are not suggestions. Apply all of them.

1. **Dollar-cost average (DCA) in.** Do not put your entire allocation in on one day. Buy in 3–4 equal purchases spread over 2–4 weeks. This smooths out your entry price.

2. **Set a hard mental stop.** If your crypto position drops 30–40% from your entry and the fundamental reason you bought it has changed, reassess and consider exiting. Do not just hold and hope indefinitely.

3. **Crypto gains are taxable in Canada.** The CRA treats crypto as a commodity. Gains are capital gains (50% inclusion rate). Every time you sell crypto for a profit, you have a taxable event. Keep records of every purchase and sale.

4. **Use Koinly.io for tax tracking.** Connect your exchange accounts. It automatically calculates your adjusted cost base and capital gains for tax filing.

5. **Keep small positions on the exchange.** For the amounts in this course ($75–$150), it is fine to keep funds on Newton or Coinbase. For larger holdings held long-term, a hardware wallet is safer.

## 5.6 How to Research a Crypto Project

Before buying any altcoin beyond BTC and ETH, answer these questions:

1. What problem does this blockchain or token actually solve?
2. Who built it? What is the team's background?
3. What is the total supply? Is there a cap? (Unlimited supply = inflation risk.)
4. What is the market cap? (Smaller market cap = more room to grow, more risk of collapse.)
5. Is there real adoption? Are people actually using this network?

If you cannot answer all five questions confidently, do not buy it.
