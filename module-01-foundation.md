# Module 01 — Foundation & Questrade Setup

**Duration:** Week 1–2 | ~2 hours total  
**Goal:** Understand the platform, know the basics, make your first trade.

---

## 1.1 Three Ways to Make Money Investing

Before touching a single stock, understand how money is actually made:

1. **Capital appreciation** — Buy at $10, sell at $20. You made $10 per share.
2. **Dividends** — Some stocks pay you cash quarterly just for holding them.
3. **Options premiums** — You collect cash from other traders for giving them a right. (Covered in Module 04.)

---

## 1.2 Questrade Account Types — Pick the Right One

### TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account) — Use This First
- All gains and withdrawals are **tax free**.
- For a beginner trying to double $500–$750, this is the most important account to use.
- Your TFSA contribution room accumulates each year. Check your CRA MyAccount to confirm your available room before depositing.

### RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan)
- Tax-deferred, not tax-free. Lower priority for this specific goal.
- Best for long-term retirement savings.

### Margin Account
- Required for options trading (Module 04).
- Open it now inside Questrade but do not fund it yet.
- Navigate to: Account Management → Open New Account → Margin.

---

## 1.3 Questrade Fee Structure — Know What You Are Paying

Understanding fees is non-negotiable with a small account. Fees eat into returns fast.

| Asset Type | Fee |
|------------|-----|
| Stocks — buying | $4.95 min, $9.95 max (1 cent per share) |
| ETFs — buying | **Free** |
| ETFs — selling | $4.95–$9.95 (same as stocks) |
| Options — per leg | $9.95 + $1.00 per contract |
| Options — round trip | ~$21.90 (open + close, 1 contract) |
| Crypto | Not supported on Questrade directly |

**Key implication for options:** A round-trip options trade on one contract costs about $22. On a $75 position, that is a 29% fee hurdle before you make a single dollar. Trade options selectively.

## 1.5 Your First Real Trade

Do not start with a stock pick. Start with a practice buy to learn the mechanics.

1. Log into Questrade and navigate to the trading screen.
2. Search ticker: **XIU** (iShares S&P/TSX 60 ETF — tracks Canada's 60 largest companies).
3. Place a **limit order** (not a market order) for 1–2 shares. A limit order means you set the maximum price you will pay. The order only fills at that price or better.
4. Watch how the order fills. Check your Portfolio tab.

You have now invested. The mechanics are the same for every future trade.

---

## 1.6 Market Order vs Limit Order — Always Use Limit

- **Market order:** Buys immediately at whatever the current price is. On thinly traded stocks or ETFs, you can get filled at a worse price than expected. Avoid.
- **Limit order:** You set the price. The order only fills if the market reaches your price. Always use this.
