## 1.3 Three Ways to Make Money Investing

You likely know some of this, but it is worth making explicit:

1. **Capital appreciation** — Buy at $10, sell at $20. You made $10 per share. This is what you are hoping for with LSPD and BTC.
2. **Dividends** — Some stocks and ETFs pay you cash quarterly just for holding them. XEQT and VFV both pay small distributions.
3. **Options premiums** — You collect cash from other traders for giving them a right to buy or sell. (Covered in Module 04.)

## 1.4 Choosing Your Platform — Questrade vs Wealthsimple vs Others

You are not locked into any platform. Here is an honest comparison for your situation.

### Wealthsimple Trade

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Stock & ETF commissions** | $0 for Canadian stocks and ETFs. $0 for US stocks with a Plus subscription ($10/month), otherwise 1.5% FX fee on US trades. |
| **Options trading** | Supported. No per-contract commission — just the FX fee if trading US options. |
| **Crypto trading** | Built-in. Buy and sell BTC, ETH, and 50+ coins directly in the app. 1.5–2% spread. |
| **Account types** | TFSA, RRSP, Margin, Corporate — all available. |
| **Interface** | Clean, mobile-first. Very beginner-friendly. |
| **Best for** | Simplicity. One app for stocks, options, and crypto. No commissions on Canadian trades. |

### Questrade

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Stock commissions** | $4.95 min, $9.95 max per trade (1 cent per share). |
| **ETF commissions** | Free to buy. $4.95–$9.95 to sell. |
| **Options trading** | $9.95 + $1.00 per contract per leg. A round-trip costs ~$22 for one contract. |
| **Crypto trading** | Not supported. You need a separate platform. |
| **Account types** | TFSA, RRSP, Margin, Corporate — all available. |
| **Interface** | More complex. Desktop platform (IQ Edge) is powerful but has a learning curve. |
| **Best for** | Serious traders who want advanced charting and order types. Better options platform. |

### Interactive Brokers (IBKR)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Stock commissions** | $0.01/share, $1.00 minimum. Very low. |
| **Options commissions** | $1.00–$1.50 per contract. Much cheaper than Questrade. |
| **Crypto trading** | Supported for BTC and ETH (through Paxos). |
| **Account types** | TFSA, RRSP, Margin — all available for Canadian residents. |
| **Interface** | Complex. Trader Workstation (TWS) is powerful but intimidating. Mobile app is cleaner. |
| **Best for** | Lowest fees overall, especially for options. Worth considering once trading volume increases. |

### Recommendation for Your Situation

**If you value simplicity:** Wealthsimple Trade. Zero commissions on Canadian stocks, built-in crypto, clean interface. The 1.5% FX fee on US trades is the main downside — but for small US positions, the convenience may be worth it.

**If you plan to trade options seriously:** Questrade or IBKR. Both have more robust options platforms. IBKR has significantly lower options fees.

**There is no wrong answer here.** You can also use multiple platforms — for example, Wealthsimple for stocks/ETFs and a dedicated crypto exchange for better crypto pricing.

## 1.5 Account Types — You Already Have the Right Ones

Since you already have TFSAs and an RRSP, here is a quick refresher on how to use them strategically:

### TFSA — Your Growth Account
- All gains are **tax-free**. This is your most valuable account for active investing.
- Put your highest-growth, highest-conviction positions here — individual stocks, aggressive ETFs.
- Do not waste TFSA room on GICs or savings accounts if you have a long time horizon.

### RRSP — Your Tax-Deferred Account
- Best for: US dividend stocks (avoids 15% withholding tax), long-term holds, conservative growth.
- Keep your diversified, low-to-medium risk holdings here. This is already what you are doing.

### Margin Account (Open One If You Do Not Have One)
- Required for options trading on most platforms.
- Also useful for any holdings that do not fit in your registered accounts.
- No need to fund it yet — just have it available.

## 1.6 Fee Awareness — Small Account, Big Impact

With positions in the $100–300 range, fees matter a lot. A $10 commission on a $200 position is a 5% fee hurdle before you make a dollar.

**Key fee implications:**
- On Wealthsimple, Canadian stock and ETF trades are free — this is a significant advantage for small positions.
- On Questrade, buying ETFs is free but selling costs $4.95–$9.95. Stock trades cost $4.95–$9.95 each way.
- For options: Questrade charges ~$22 round-trip per contract. IBKR charges ~$3. On a $100 options trade, that is the difference between a 22% fee hurdle and a 3% fee hurdle.
