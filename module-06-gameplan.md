## 6.1 Portfolio Allocation

Here is the suggested split for your $500–$750 starting capital. This balances growth potential against the reality of protecting a small account.

| Allocation | Asset Type | Amount (on $750) | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| 40% | Core ETFs | $300 | Safety anchor. Steady growth. Low maintenance. |
| 35% | Individual stocks | $262 | Growth engine. 2–3 high-conviction picks from your research. |
| 15% | Options | $112 | Leverage layer. 1–2 trades per month maximum. |
| 10% | Crypto | $75 | Asymmetric upside. High risk, small size. |

### Specific ETF Allocation Suggestion
- XEQT or XIU in your TFSA for the ETF portion.
- Buy ETFs first. They are free to purchase on Questrade and require no ongoing decisions.

### Specific Stock Allocation Suggestion
- Maximum 2–3 individual stocks.
- No single stock should exceed 15% of your total account ($112 on $750).
- All stocks should have passed the 5-Point Screen from Module 02.

## 6.2 Weekly Routine — 1–3 Hours Per Week

Consistency beats intensity. Use this structure every week.

### Sunday (30–45 minutes)
- Review your full portfolio. Check current prices against your entry prices.
- Ask: has anything changed about why I own each position?
- Check TradingView for chart updates on each holding.
- Note anything unusual (earnings announcements, news) for the coming week.
- Decide if any planned trades are ready to execute.

### Tuesday or Wednesday (30–60 minutes)
- Research. Read one company's earnings report or analyst note.
- Run a new stock through the 5-Point Screen.
- Watch one educational video from the resources list.

### As needed (15–30 minutes)
- Execute trades you planned on Sunday. Not before.
- If a trade idea comes to you mid-week and was not planned Sunday, write it down and evaluate it next Sunday. Impulsive trades are almost always worse than planned trades.

A 100% return in 12 months is possible. It requires strong stock picks, at least a few successful options trades, and a favorable crypto environment. It is not guaranteed. In a bad market environment, preserving capital and learning is the real win for year one.

## 6.4 Common Mistakes That Kill Small Accounts

| Mistake | What It Looks Like | How to Avoid It |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| Chasing momentum | Buying a stock after it already went up 40% because it appeared on Reddit. | By the time retail investors hear about it, the move is usually over. Only buy based on your own research and chart setup. |
| Panic selling | Selling when a stock drops 10–15% without the fundamentals changing. | Set your stop loss before you buy. If price has not hit your stop, hold your plan. |
| Overconcentration | Putting 50%+ of your account into one stock or crypto. | Follow the allocation table. No single position above 15% of total account. |
| Ignoring fees | Not accounting for Questrade's $22 round-trip on options trades. | Calculate fee impact before every options trade. Small positions often cannot overcome the fee hurdle. |
| No stop losses | Having no predefined exit if wrong. | Write your stop loss price in `progress-tracker.md` before placing every order. |
| Trading on emotions | Buying because you are excited. Selling because you are scared. | Only execute trades that were planned with a clear head. |
| Overtrading | Making 10+ trades per month trying to catch every move. | More trades = more fees + more mistakes. Aim for 2–4 considered trades per month maximum. |

## 6.5 Monthly Portfolio Review Checklist

Do this at the end of every month:

- [ ] Calculate total portfolio value. What is your return so far?
- [ ] Review every open position. Does your thesis still hold for each?
- [ ] Review every closed trade. What worked? What did not? Why?
- [ ] Check allocation percentages. Have any positions grown too large? Trim if needed.
- [ ] Set goals for the next month. What will you research? What setups are you watching?
- [ ] Update `progress-tracker.md` with your monthly summary.

## 6.6 Tax Considerations for Canadian Investors

- **TFSA gains:** Not taxable at all. Maximize use of this account.
- **Margin account gains (stocks):** Capital gains — 50% of the gain is included in taxable income.
- **Options gains:** Also capital gains treatment in most cases. Consult a tax professional for complex situations.
- **Crypto gains:** Capital gains — 50% inclusion rate. Track every transaction in Koinly.io.
- **US stocks in a non-registered account:** Subject to a 15% withholding tax on US dividends. Hold US dividend stocks in an RRSP to avoid this.

## 6.7 When to Reassess the Strategy

At the 6-month mark, evaluate honestly:

1. What is your total return so far?
2. Which part of the strategy is working (ETFs, stocks, options, crypto)?
3. Which part is not working? Why?
4. Should you shift allocation toward what is working and away from what is not?
5. Do you have enough knowledge now to take on more complexity (e.g., more options strategies)?

The course is a starting framework. It is meant to be adapted based on your real results.

## Final Principle

The market rewards the prepared and the patient. Every loss is a lesson if you record what happened and why. Every gain is a signal to understand what you did right and whether it was skill or luck.

Track everything. Learn from everything. The goal in year one is not just to double your money — it is to become someone who knows how to invest. That knowledge compounds for the rest of your life.
